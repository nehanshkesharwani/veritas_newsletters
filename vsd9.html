🧠"Umm… Like… You Know?" – The Hidden Cost of Filler Words

🗣️Umm… Like… You Know?

Have you ever said something like this?
"So… umm… I was, like, thinking that we could, you know… maybe try a different… uh… approach?"

If yes, you're not alone.
If no — you probably didn't notice yourself doing it.

Most of us use filler words unconsciously. We say uh, um, like, you know, actually, basically, literally — not to communicate, but to fill silence.

But here's the thing: Every filler word weakens your authority — just a little bit.
And over time, those "little bits" add up.

🎯Why We Use Filler Words
  • Fear of Silence
    Silence feels awkward, so we rush to fill it.
  • Processing Thoughts
    Our brain hasn't caught up to our mouth, so we buy time using "um" and "like."
  • Lack of Awareness
    Most people don't even realize they're using fillers — until they hear themselves recorded.
🛠️How to Fix It
  • 1. Record Yourself
    Yes, again. Awareness begins with observation. Record a 1-minute explanation of anything — and play it back. Count the filler words.
  • 2. Replace Fillers with Pauses
    Instead of saying, "So um… let me explain this again…", try: "So… let me explain this again." That one-second pause is your new super-power.
  • 3. Practice the "Slow Start" Drill
    Before any speech or presentation, take 5 slow deep breaths. Then start slowly and clearly — pace is everything.
💡 Golden Point: Every time you pause instead of saying "umm," you sound 10× more confident.
🔁 Challenge of the Week

Record a 1-minute answer to this prompt:
"Why is communication important in my field?"

  • Count how many fillers you used
  • Try again, consciously removing them

🎯 Goal: Reduce your filler count by 50 % between both takes.

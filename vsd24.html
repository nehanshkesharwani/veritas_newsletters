1 What Are Rhetorical Questions?

They're questions you don't expect an answer to‚Äîbut they make people stop, think, and connect with your message.

"What would you attempt if you knew you couldn't fail?"

Such questions shift the focus to the audience, making them reflect rather than passively listen.

2 Why Do They Work?
Activate the brain: Questions grab attention because our minds naturally want to answer.
Create curiosity: A thought-provoking question builds suspense and keeps listeners hooked.
Add rhythm: They break the monotony of long statements and make the delivery conversational.
3 Where to Use Them
Openers: Start with a bold question to immediately pull your audience in.
"Why do some people succeed while others don't?"
Transitions: Use questions to shift topics smoothly.
"But what happens when everything goes wrong?"
Closings: Leave the audience thinking with a powerful final question.
"What will you do differently starting today?"
4 How to Master Them
Keep them short and impactful.

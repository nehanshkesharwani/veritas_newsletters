üß†The Real Cause of "Authority Anxiety"

Fear of authority is often not about what to say but how we feel while saying it.

  • We overprepare.
  • We obsess about every pause, word, and gesture.
  • We treat the conversation like a judgment, not a dialogue.

The key is to shift focus from trying to be perfect to allowing yourself to be authentic.

4 Steps to Overcome Speaking Pressure
  • Identify Your Inner Critic

    Notice the negative voices: "What if I mess up?"

    Acknowledge them: "This is just my mind trying to trip me up. It's not the truth."

    Labeling these thoughts removes their power.

  • Normalize Your Nerves

    Feeling pressure means the moment is important‚Äîand that's okay.

    Instead of panicking, remind yourself: "Everyone feels nervous before big conversations. This is normal."
  • Pivot Your Focus

    Overthinking only fuels anxiety.

    Shift your attention: Listen to music, watch something funny, or do breathing exercises.

    Try the 5-minute breathing drill: Focus only on your inhale and exhale. It calms your mind before any meeting.
  • Reframe Your Perception

    What you expect is what you project.

    Stop thinking "I'll mess this up."

    Replace it with "I know my topic. I'm here to share, not prove myself."

The Art of Introducing Yourself

Think about the last time you met someone new ‚Äî in class, during an event, or while waiting in the cafeteria.

Most people simply say:

"Hi, I'm Rahul, I'm in finance."
"Hello, I'm Neha, I run a digital agency."
"Hey, I'm Sameer, I'm into analytics."

And just like that, they're forgotten.

Why? Because these introductions sound like labels ‚Äî flat, predictable, and forgettable.

Why Good Introductions Matter Everywhere

Introductions aren't just for interviews or jobs. They shape first impressions every time you meet someone. Whether you're at a seminar, talking to a professor, or meeting a stranger at a fest, your introduction decides if someone will:

  • Remember your name, or
  • Forget you in the next 30 seconds

You don't get a second chance to make that first impression.

The 3C Method for Introductions

Here's a simple, natural way to introduce yourself so people remember you:

Context

Start with where you're coming from or why you're here.

Character

Add something that shows your personality, interest, or passion.

Connection

End with a line that sparks curiosity or opens conversation.

Examples (Casual & Real)
Instead of:
"Hi, I'm Ankit, I'm studying engineering."
Say:
"Hi, I'm Ankit. I'm from Delhi, and I love building things ‚Äî last month I made a mini drone for fun. Are you into tech too?"
Instead of:
"Hello, I'm Sneha, I'm in computer science."
Say:
"Hi, I'm Sneha. I'm always chasing ways to make life easier with tech ‚Äî recently, I created a small app for my hostel. What about you, what's keeping you busy?"

This doesn't sound rehearsed. It feels real and approachable.

Challenge of the Week

Rewrite your introduction using the 3C Method:

  • Context: Where you're from or what you're doing right now.
  • Character: One thing that makes you interesting.
  • Connection: Something that invites conversation.

Practice with friends and see if they remember it after 5 minutes.

Key Takeaways
  • Generic intros get lost.
  • Show your personality, not your title.
  • Create a hook that makes people curious.

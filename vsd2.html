"Be brief. Be clear. Be remembered."
Welcome to this week's edition! Learn how to introduce yourself in a way that makes people remember you — not just your job title.

🔔Don't Introduce Yourself Like This

Picture this.

You're at a seminar, club meet, or networking event. You turn to someone next to you, and they say:

"Hi, I'm Rahul. I work in finance."
"Hello, I'm Neha. I run a digital agency."
"Hey, I'm Sameer. I'm into analytics."

A polite smile. A nod.
And three minutes later? You've already forgotten their names.

Why does this happen?

Because these introductions tell us almost nothing. They sound rehearsed, impersonal, and too vague to stick.

❓Why Generic Intros Don't Work

When you say "I'm in marketing," most people file you away in a mental folder and move on.
Worse — if they're unsure what that means, they won't even ask.

The truth is:
If your intro sounds like everyone else's, you'll be forgotten like everyone else.

🛠️Use This 4-Part Formula Instead

Here's a simple introduction format that makes people curious, not confused:

  • What You Do — Be clear: what result do you create or problem do you solve?
  • Who You Help — Mention the type of people you work with or serve
  • How You Do It — Add a simple method, tool, or example
  • Why It Matters — Highlight the outcome or reason this work matters
🎯Example (General Public-Speaking Version)
"I help college students become better speakers by training them to think clearly, speak confidently, and express ideas that stick — because in today's world, you're remembered for how well you speak."

This doesn't sound corporate or robotic.
It sounds real. Relatable. Memorable.

💬Challenge of the Week

Recraft your own intro using this formula.
Practice it with a friend. Ask them:

  • Did they understand what you do?
  • Would they remember you?
  • Would they want to hear more?
📦Recap:
✅ Be specific about what you do
✅ Mention who you help
✅ Share how you do it
✅ Add a reason that matters

🔄 TL;DR: Vague = forgettable. Clear + Relevant = memorable.

The goal isn't to impress people.
It's to connect with them — and be remembered for the right reasons.

"You never get a second chance to make a first impression."
⭐ Golden Rule: Be the intro they talk about — not the one they forget.
